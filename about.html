Ed McMorrow was born and raised in Chimacum, Washington. He has been a Registered Piano Technician since 1971 and a member of the Seattle Chapter of the Piano Technicians Guild since 1974. Over the course of more than fifty years, he has held the most demanding positions in the profession: piano technician for the Seattle Symphony for thirteen years and head technician at Seattle's Sherman Clay Steinway dealership for sixteen.

Ed pushes the boundaries of what a piano can become. His LightHammer Tone Regulation provides dynamic tone and ergonomic touch by adjusting hammer weight in relation to action leverage, nearly eliminating key weights. His Hybrid Wire Scaling techniques, using softer wires from makers like Paulello and Pure Sound, greatly improve scale breaks. His Fully Tempered Duplex Scale, a U.S. patent-pending innovation, controls harmonic interactions from longitudinal and transverse modes to provide clarity and a singing tone across the keyboard compass.

The Piano Technicians Journal reviewed his book, The Educated Piano, favorably in December 1989 and featured his Fully Tempered Duplex Scale in the March 2013 issue. The PTJ called his tone regulation work "particularly profound."

Ed has taught at many national and regional PTG conventions. His most recent appearance was at the 2024 PTG National Convention in Reno, Nevada, as part of the Rebuilders Showcase. His classes feature groundbreaking piano service protocols, including LightHammer Tone Regulation, Hybrid Wire Scaling, and rebuilding of unique instruments like the Fender Rhodes.

Within the Seattle Chapter, Ed served as Chapter President from 2018 to 2022 and continues as a chapter officer. He hosts workshops and local events, frequently presents at meetings for the Seattle Chapter and nearby chapters, trains apprentices, and has helped many young technicians grow in their craft through mentoring and sponsorship via the Seattle PTG Scholarship Program.

Ed works closely with local music associations, including EMTA and WSMTA, organizing events and recitals in his piano showroom. He composes music for piano and voice, often singing his compositions while working on an instrument. He has been married for forty-five years.

Every piano Ed rebuilds is engineered and customized to bring out the highest possible capability of that specific instrument. His work is singular in the field.
